The opportunity

Big feelings are normal. Adults can make the difference.

Preschoolers often experience emotions before they have the words or tools to manage them. With steady support, hard moments can become opportunities to build emotional literacy.

A practical approach

A simple sequence for difficult moments

Training turns a helpful instinct into an approach educators and families can use consistently.

  1. 01

    Pause

    Notice the feeling behind the behavior before reacting.

  2. 02

    Connect

    Offer calm presence and name what may be happening.

  3. 03

    Practice

    Build simple regulation skills when the child is ready to learn.

About Susan

Experience that connects the classroom and the family.

Susan C. Gann brings together a Master’s in Early Childhood Education, a Master’s in Mental Health from the Johns Hopkins School of Medicine, and decades of experience as a child psychotherapist and parent counselor.

Today, she serves as Parent Advisor and a board member at The New School of Berkeley, where she has also trained staff in emotional intelligence.
